Receptor Roullette

This game illustrates T Cell specification.

Three types of cells will be presented on the screen.

  • Functional "killer" T Cells; these bind to MHC I (thanks to their CD8 co-receptors) and recognize a specific peptide.
  • Functional "helper" T Cells; these bind to MHC II instead of MHC I (since they have CD4 co-receptors, not CD8) and still recognize a specific peptide.
  • Double negative T Cells; these are nonfunctional, having neither CD8 nor CD4 co-receptors.
  • An antigen presenting cell; this cell represents any number of cells a T Cell might come across with MHC I displaying a peptide on its surface.

During T Cell maturation, several things happen.

  • Killer T Cells are tested for peptide specificity by binding to MHC I on a professional APC.
  • Helper T Cells are treated the same, but with an MHC II APC. There is no such APC in the game.
  • Apoptosis is induced in double negative T Cells.
Mirror this process in the game as quickly as you can! (Hints: double tap a cell to induce apoptosis; we are only using one APC, so there will be more cells than you'll be able to deal with, but don't worry because this won't affect your score.)

Some things to think about once you enter the game:

  • Where is the APC?
  • Which cells are killer T Cells?
  • Which cells are helper T Cells?
  • Which cells are double negative?
